Key planning decisions (updated June 2026)

  1. Ethics is submitted by the end of September 2026 with the full instrument package (protocol, survey, interview and focus group guides, consent and information sheets). The 17 June meeting moved the target earlier, from October or November to end September. Stefano has a route around the instruments up front constraint, and the instruments are finalised with him in early September. Submission goes to UM REC through FREC, the Faculty of Arts committee, plus the three in-country bodies. This still leaves five to six months for review cycles, corrections, and in-country processes before the April 2027 departure. March 2027 remains a deliberate buffer.
  2. Both WP2 teaching seminars are delivered virtually within semester one. The ISSI MA host seminar (in Pedro's unit) lands in the second week of October, and the Hildesheim guest seminar on 27 November. Both sit well before M.2.1 (training complete, February), which therefore carries no risk. Virtual delivery also reduces the project's carbon footprint, in line with the MSCA Green Charter.
  3. Relationship building opens now (June), not October. Comoros goes first, in French, as the coldest start, then warm introductions to Jamaica and Tonga. October remains the milestone for formally named counterparts (D.3.2).
  4. The MOOC is a slow burn: outline in October, scripts through November and December, recording only in January.
  5. The September data pull extracts every series back to 1980 (for the year two ARDL models); the proposal's 2000 to 2020 review window is a subset of the same extraction.
  6. Deliverable and milestone dates from the accepted Gantt never move; only the work behind them starts earlier.
  7. Workshops are semester bound. The Doctoral School Semester 1 begins 1 October, so the four taught workshops (D.2.1 to D.2.4) are scheduled across October to December, more than one a month where it helps. The FinTech elective (D.2.5) is now a self-paced Coursera course from the Inter-American Development Bank, taken in August and reimbursed through an RTN memo to the Project Support Office.

The seven work packages in plain language

WP1, Project management (all 24 months). The paperwork spine. D.1.1 is the opening bundle (mobility declaration, PCDP, training plan); D.1.2 is the data management plan plus the digital platform (project page, OSF, Dataverse, GitHub, with the GitHub page live in June or July), updated annually; D.1.3 to D.1.10 are trimestral progress reports with quarterly PCDP updates.

WP2, Training at ISSI (months 1 to 9). Four workshops scheduled across October to December (quantitative methods, policy writing, leadership and project management, integrated methods), the FinTech elective now taken as the Coursera IDB financial inclusion course, two teaching seminars designed and delivered with authentic assessments (both virtual, semester one), and MOOC 1, Bridging Cultures with Reciprocity.

WP3, Fieldwork preparation, remote from Malta (year 1). Language practice (Tongan and Shikomori), formal partnerships with UWI Jamaica, USP Tonga, and the University of Comoros (warm emails from June, Comoros first), study site selection with community stakeholders, ethics (scheduled here as an internal addition, now submitted end September), and offering MOOC 1 at partner universities.

WP4, Ethnographic fieldwork (April to October 2027). Roughly two months per country in the order Jamaica, Tonga, Comoros, chosen to avoid peak cyclone and hurricane seasons. Within each visit the sequence is ethnography first, surveys after, since interviews inform the survey in practice. Per country KPI: 10 to 20 interviews, 3 focus groups, 250 to 300 household surveys. Each visit ends with a secondment at the local university.

WP5, Econometrics and analysis (brackets the fieldwork). Before: the gender sensitive monitoring framework (D.5.1) and the systematic review of World Bank and OECD data (M.5.1), supported by the household survey literature reading that begins in June. After: design three site specific ARDL models with Stefano (D.5.2), then analyse data using the structured framework (D.5.3).

WP6, Two way knowledge transfer. Four research seminars (ISSI plus one at each partner university during secondments), two international conference presentations, and MOOC 2, Waves of Wealth.

WP7, Dissemination and exploitation. One local newspaper or magazine article per country timed to fieldwork, policy briefs for SIDS governments, donors, and regional bodies, three journal article submissions, and the MSCA evaluation questionnaire at month 24. The social media project (Island Voices, Global Impact) is set up and begins building content now, with the formal launch milestone M.3.1 staying in March 2027. WP7 closes with D.7.7.
