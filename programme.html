The work programme

Seven work packages

The fellowship is organised into seven work packages, each with its own deliverables (D) and milestones (M). The dates have shifted as the project has taken shape, but every deliverable remains.

Work Package 1

Project management

Active nowAll 24 months

The administrative backbone of the fellowship: the opening paperwork, the data and platform setup, and regular reporting.

Deliverables and milestone D.1.1 Mobility declaration, PCDP, and training plan.   D.1.2 Data management plan and digital platform (this website, the EU Open Research Repository on Zenodo, OAR@UM, and GitHub).   D.1.3 to D.1.10 Trimestral progress reports and quarterly PCDP updates.
Milestone M.1.1 · PCDP, DMP, and platform complete (Dec 2026)
Work Package 2

Training at ISSI

Active nowMonths 1 to 9

Advanced training to build the project's interdisciplinary toolkit, delivered across Semester 1.

Deliverables and milestones D.2.1 to D.2.4 Four doctoral workshops (quantitative methods, policy writing, leadership and project management, integrated methods), now scheduled October to December, with the statistics groundwork for the quantitative strand laid early through the University of Malta IT Services IBM SPSS courses (Introduction and Intermediate) in July 2026.   D.2.5 FinTech elective, taken as a Coursera course.   D.2.6 ISS5200 teaching seminar within the ISSI MA in October, about four hours across one week with an interactive assessment.   D.2.7 Hildesheim guest seminar on 27 November.   D.2.8 MOOC 1, Bridging Cultures with Reciprocity.
Milestone M.2.1 · training complete (Feb 2027)   M.2.2 · final database in open access (2028)
Work Package 3

Fieldwork preparation

Active nowYear 1, remote from Malta

Getting ready for the field: language, partnerships, study sites, and the ethics package, now prepared for submission by the end of August. Relationship building opened in June, with interest confirmed across all three sites.

Deliverables and milestones D.3.1 Language practice (Tongan and Shikomori).   D.3.2 Partnerships with UWI, USP Tonga, and the University of Comoros, now under way.   D.3.3 Study site selection.   D.3.4 Offer MOOC 1 to partner universities.
Milestone M.3.1 · project social media launched (Mar 2027)   M.3.2 · languages, partnerships, and sites complete
Work Package 4

Ethnographic fieldwork

Apr to Oct 2027

Roughly two months in each country in turn. Ethnography first, surveys after, with every visit closing in a secondment.

Deliverables and milestone D.4.1 to D.4.3 Data collection in Jamaica, Tonga, and Comoros (10 to 20 interviews, 3 focus groups, 250 to 300 surveys each).   D.4.4 to D.4.6 Secondments at the partner universities.
Milestone M.4.1 · fieldwork and secondments complete (Oct 2027)
Work Package 5

Econometrics and analysis

Active nowBrackets the fieldwork

The quantitative engine, from the systematic data review to the country level models.

Deliverables and milestones D.5.1 Gender sensitive monitoring framework.   D.5.2 Three site specific ARDL models.   D.5.3 Analysis using the Sustainable Livelihoods Framework.
Milestone M.5.1 · review of World Bank and OECD data (Nov 2026)   M.5.2 · modelling and comparison complete (2028)
Work Package 6

Two-way knowledge transfer

Across the two years

Sharing the work in both directions, with partner communities and the wider research field.

Deliverables and milestone D.6.1 to D.6.4 Four research seminars (ISSI, UWI, USP Tonga, University of Comoros).   D.6.5, D.6.6 International conference presentations, now mapped to the project's three papers: a systematic review at DevNet 2026 in Wellington (December), a methodology paper with preliminary findings at the Adaptation Futures Conference in Cancún (2027), and the results at the ISISA Islands of the World Congress in Gozo (2028), with the ISISA congress in Curaçao (June 2027) a further opportunity for early findings.   D.6.7 MOOC 2, Waves of Wealth.
Milestone M.6.1 · knowledge transfer complete (May 2028)
Work Package 7

Dissemination and exploitation

Active nowA living plan, to May 2028

Making sure the findings reach the people they are about, from local press to policy and journals.

Deliverables D.7.1 to D.7.3 A local newspaper or magazine article in each country.   D.7.4 Policy briefs for governments, donors, and regional bodies.   D.7.5 to D.7.7 Three journal articles, now defined: a PRISMA systematic review that links the econometrics to the wider field, a methodology paper, and the results, alongside the MSCA evaluation questionnaire.
No milestone in the accepted plan; the package closes with D.7.7
